Introduction back to ToC

The GENIAL! Basic Ontology (GBO) was designed to describe the areas of systems engineering and electronic development. The goal is to be able to consistenly describe systems and their parts in a physical way, but also with all its related factors for their development. Thus their properties, their functions, requirements and their dependencies can also be described. GBO is built upon two features. The first one includes the incorporation and usage of the Basic Formal Ontology (BFO). All concepts are aligned with BFO and BFO's design principles are consistently applied to GBO and the knowledge base content that is created with GBO. Further GBO is built according to the ISO26262 standard in automotive electronic safety, which makes electronic safety and automotive development a related topic. The second feature is a heavyweight approach to ontology modelling. The definitions of ISO26262 have been formalized meticulously and are available for reasoning purposes. GBO is intended to be specifically used with reasoners in order to improve the overall quality of knowlege bases or knowledge graphs built with GBO. This is a different approach than how most ontologies are currently built. The goal is to build an extensive high quality knowledge base of systems and electronic engineering. One application, but not limited to it, is the exchange of knowledge in the automotive value chain. Key terms to describe physical systems are: system, hardware, software, component, hardware component, hardware part, hardware subpart, hardware elementary subpart, software component and software unit. GENIAL! Basic Ontology: Description back to ToC

Basic Ontology with general vocabulary for serving as a ground to execute basic tasks of the tool AGILA and share common upper level for all classes in GENIAL!. It supports the modelling of microlectronic components like sensors, acutators, processors and software along the automotive value chain.
