Ontology Specification Draft

Abstract

This ontology serves as a standard to employ consciousness technologies for modern computer systems. The basis is that everything in the universe emits an energy pattern that can be read throughout time and space. These energy patterns are represented in levels according to the Map of ConsciousnessÂ© by Dr. David R. Hawkins. They have been encoded in the Ontology Web Language (OWL) to support computer readability, processing and understandability. Further some basic axioms are formulated to support reasoning capabilities.

Introduction back to ToC

The basic building block of this ontology is the "object of Truth." This includes everything. It can be simple objects like movies, books, persons, animals, places, locations, art, sculptures, software etc. But can also be events, activities, qualitites, functions or more complex artifacts. For example the happening of an event may span across certain countries. But you want to look at an event in a particular country. In this case the "object of Truth" needs to be defined with context and multiple triples in the knowledge base. The second most important class is the class "levels of consciousness." It relates a quantity to the object under consideration ("object of Truth"). This holds in both directions which improves database queries.
(PDF) An OWL Ontology of Consciousness. Available from: https://www.researchgate.net/publication/383227961_An_OWL_Ontology_of_Consciousness [accessed Aug 24 2024].

The other parts of the ontology give a contextualization to the core of the ontology. It is the ego of the person who perceives about the content of reality, but is oblivious to the essence of context or what something actually means. The basic conundrum of the human mind, which is unable to distinguish Truth from falsehood. Each level of consciousness is further formalized with its energy.

Fig. 1. gives an outline of the core of the ontology.

The Map of Consciousness represents energy levels from 0-1000. The map is logarithmic and has equivalents in energies of thought patterns in micro watts. Lower levels represent weak energy patterns that are based on force. Whereas energy levels above 200 are based on power signify truth, courage and integrity and a stronger influence. Each level comes together with prevalent emotions, a specific world view, a god view as well as its own process. This explains how disagreements in society arise. Since perspectives, goals, intentions, actions and so forth are different and sometimes in conflict with each other. They are determined by their according energy level. 200 is the critical level that distinguishes truth from falsehood or integrity from non-integrity. Above 200 and beyond the levels become progressively benign. At the level of 400 at reason, the process is understanding and therefore a more effective functioning of society (or the "object of Truth") is possible. This further develops to the point of unity (500) and oneness (600) where harmony and good will towards everything and a higher degree of wisdom are common place. The quantified values have been represented in the ontology with their qualitative levels. For a thorough explanation of the levels see the books Power vs Force and Truth vs. Falsehood by Dr. David Hawkins.

OWL Ontology of Consciousness: Description back to ToC

The OWL Ontology of Consciousness is a consolidation of a general theory of consciousness. It serves as a standard to describe, use and apply consciousness in modern computer systems. The unified data model can be imported whenever consciousness technologies need to be incorporated by users, researchers, scientists or the general public.

IRI: https://www.w3id.org/people/wawrzik/ooc#object_of_truth

An "object of Truth" is content (a non-contextual entity) that is defined by what the entity means or is and the context that defines the entity. An "object of Truth" is as content related to a contexttual factor or proximate field. But specfically characterized from all other entities (e.g. a bfo:entity) in that it is related to a level of consciousness.

E.g. a person, a book, a movie, an event, a treatment, a software program, an organization, a country or a statement
